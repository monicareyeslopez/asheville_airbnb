Author: Monica Reyes-Lopez

This document provides a step-by-step account of the analytical techniques, tools, and reasoning used to study the Asheville, NC Airbnb market using InsideAirbnb data. Four primary techniques were employed: Random Forest regression for occupancy prediction, SARIMA time-series forecasting of review volume, VADER sentiment analysis on guest reviews, and LDA topic modeling to surface recurring themes in guest language. 

My findings converge on a single theme: **the host matters more than the home**. Contrary to expectations, neighborhood location ranked below pricing strategy and host professionalism as occupancy drivers. This result is grounded in a Random Forest model (R² = 0.842) where Price and Host Listings Count emerged as the top two predictors. Complementing the predictive modeling, VADER Sentiment Analysis and LDA Topic Modeling on guest reviews revealed that visitors are overwhelmingly drawn to Asheville as a destination, with location experience dominating review topics. SARIMA forecasting of monthly review volume further contextualized demand trends over time. Review data was chosen for its longest historical span across datasets. Together, these techniques paint a picture of a market where strategic hospitality practices, not property attributes, define success.
